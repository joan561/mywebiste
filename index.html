Skincare and Aesthetics

Introduction

My sensitive skin has taken me through various points and feelings in my life as a typical teenage girl. At some point in my teen life I felt very insecure about my skin. I realized the problem was never my skin, it was the way I treated it. 

Skincare involves a daily routine of cleaning, mosturizing and sun protection to improve and maintain skin health. A basic routine includes washing your face twice daily, applying a moisturizer suitable for your skin type, and using sunscreen every day to protect against sun damage.

Here are some facts that I never knew affected skin health:

  • A lack of sleep can affect your skin.
  • Genetics
  • Hormonal changes can trigger the acne of puberty.
  • Poor food choices can affect the perfomace of skin health.
  • Stress and anxiety trigger breakouts.

What are 5 Basics of skincare?

  • Tips: find a brand that works perfectly for your skin. Your skin is not supposed to burn after using a product. I thought the burning sensation meant the product is working, however that is not the case.
  • Identify your skin type

1. Cleanse your skin with water and micellar water to achieve a deep cleansing effect

2. Use a facewash that works for you and stay consistent to that facewash.

3. Apply a cream in the morning and at night with effective skin protection.

4. Apply a light serum for skin moisture.

5. Use a suncreen everyday to protect your skin from the sun and UV rays.

Common skincare myths to avoid:

  • Expensive is better: The effectiveness of a product is based on its active ingredients and formulation, not its price tag.
  • Oily skin doesn't need moisturizer: Skipping moisturizer can cause your skin to produce more oil to compensate for dehydration, leading to more clogged pores and breakouts.
  • You only need sunscreen on sunny days: UV rays can penetrate clouds and glass, so daily sunscreen application is essential for preventing sun damage and signs of aging.
  • The more products, the better: Overloading your skin can lead to irritation. A simpler, consistent routine is often more effective.
